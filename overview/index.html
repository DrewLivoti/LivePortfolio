Four moves, across four contexts.

Programs where the differentiating decision was architecture, not execution. Each represents a category — AI tooling, lifecycle revenue, partnerships, B2B engineering — and a different industry.

01 / AI & Internal Tooling Pfizer · 2024

Built solo. Used by the entire division.

  • Built a CRM process AI agent solo on Pfizer's GenAI platform (Claude + GPT engines) — no engineering team, no vendor
  • Used today across 3 workstream handoffs — production, ops, data science — with adoption spanning peers, brand leads, and Directors above me
  • 12-week process compressed to 10 weeks when the agent is used effectively across those handoffs

The moveSaw the 12-team handoff process as the bottleneck and embedded the institutional knowledge into an agent — instead of writing more documentation.

02 / Lifecycle Revenue Pfizer · 2024

$15M from an unexpected project.

  • Architected the abandoned-cart program to trigger from SAP natively, not from the marketing automation stack
  • Unlocked promotional classification + 100% reach (bypassed marketing opt-outs) + every-5-min deployment vs. SFMC's 1-per-day cap
  • Internal pre-launch estimate: $10–12M annualized. Actual: ~$15M.

The moveRouted around the obvious tool. The marketing automation queue would have capped us; SAP-native triggers gave us 5-minute latency and full deliverability.

03 / Partnerships & Engagement Akili · 2023

30% engagement from a C-suite friendship.

  • Noticed Akili's CEO had a personal friendship with Roblox's CEO — pitched and closed the partnership myself
  • Designed an in-game hat economy unlocked by EndeavorRx app progression. Owned segmentation + SFMC plumbing + brand brief
  • 30% engagement lift measured against control by data science, tracked daily in Looker

The moveSaw a relationship the org wasn't using and converted it into a discovery channel — bringing new audiences while lifting retention for existing kids playing the app.

04 / Engineering & B2B Ops Nikon · 2018

12 emails into one, at midnight.

  • New-product launch emails to vendor lists (Walmart, Target, Best Buy, etc.) had to ship at midnight to prevent leaks — hackers actively monitored production folders
  • Built assets in proofing folders with production references, "moved" them at midnight so files instantly resolved with full personalization + dynamic content
  • Consolidated 12 vendor-specific emails into 1 dynamic-content send. Eliminated a 2-person 12am–2am shift and closed the leak vector.

The moveSolved a security problem and an ops problem at once — by changing the build pipeline, not the launch process.
